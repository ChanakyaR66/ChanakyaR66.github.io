About

Turning data into
smarter risk decisions

I hold a Master's in Business Analytics from UT Dallas, where I specialised in the financial analytics track — studying credit risk modelling, portfolio analytics, and the quantitative frameworks that sit behind real lending decisions. That academic foundation shaped how I think about data: not as an end in itself, but as the input to decisions that have real financial consequences.

I worked on the credit card risk team at Citigroup, where I analysed delinquency patterns, supported model validation cycles, and collaborated with ML engineering to keep risk models performing as borrower behaviour shifts. The work demands both technical depth and business clarity — knowing when a model metric matters and when it doesn't.

Before Citigroup I spent two years at Bhanzu, a venture-backed edtech startup, working closely with the COO's office on payment funnel analytics and retention strategy. Working with payment data at a fast-growing company taught me how to move quickly, communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders, and build things that actually get used.

I'm currently preparing for FRM Level 1 to sharpen the theoretical foundations underneath the practical work — risk measurement, credit products, and the regulatory frameworks that shape how institutions lend responsibly.
